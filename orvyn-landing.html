The Challenge

Stroke doesn't erase
the will to move.

After a stroke, the brain's signal to the hand is damaged — but the intent isn't. Patients try to move. Their muscles respond, weakly. Nothing follows through.

The Problem

Paralysis of intent

Stroke patients retain the desire to move their hand, but the neural pathway to complete the movement is severed or degraded. The muscles receive a partial signal — but can't follow through on their own. Traditional rehab is slow, expensive, and repetitive without measurable feedback.

The Solution

ORVYN closes the loop

A single sEMG sensor on the inner forearm listens for that residual electrical intent. ORVYN's proportional control system translates any signal — however weak — into full, smooth finger movement via 4 servo motors and Kevlar tendons. No calibration buttons. No manual assist. Just try.

Mechanism

Three steps from
signal to motion.

01

Detect intent

The OYMotion SEN0240 surface EMG sensor reads the electrical signal from forearm muscles — even at 10% of normal strength. Signal is sampled and filtered in real time by a Teensy 4.0 microcontroller.

→
02

Proportional assist

Signal intensity maps 0–100% to motor assist level. If you're trying 40%, ORVYN assists 40%. This preserves active engagement — patients train their brain to reconnect, not just receive passive movement.

→
03

Complete the movement

Four servo motors drive Kevlar tendons across all five fingers. Full range of motion — open, partial flex, pinch, full grip — achieved within 20ms of signal detection. Smooth, anatomical motion.

Hardware

Built with precision.
Designed for rehab.

MICROCONTROLLER
Teensy 4.0
600MHz ARM Cortex-M7. Reads EMG, runs proportional control algorithm, outputs PWM to 4 servo channels simultaneously.
WIRELESS MODULE
ESP32-C WiFi
Streams live telemetry to the ORVYN dashboard over local WiFi via UART bridge. Enables real-time remote monitoring.
EMG SENSOR
OYMotion SEN0240
Surface sEMG. Single electrode placement on inner forearm. 0–3V output range. Picks up voluntary muscle signals even at severely reduced amplitude.
ACTUATORS
4× Servo + Kevlar
Dorsal A/B and Volar A/B servo channels drive ultra-light Kevlar tendons across all five fingers for anatomically natural flex and extension.
POWER
7.4V LiPo
Lightweight lithium polymer battery powers the full system — Teensy, ESP32, all 4 servos — for approximately 3 hours of active rehab use.
CONTROL ALGORITHM
Proportional Assist
No thresholds, no discrete modes. Signal strength maps linearly to assist level — 0% intent = idle, 100% intent = full grip. Response under 20ms end-to-end.

The Build

Made at university.
Designed for impact.

ORVYN was conceived, designed, and built as a university engineering project — motivated by one question: why does assistive technology for stroke patients cost as much as a car?

Every component was chosen for availability, repairability, and cost. The housing is 3D-printed. The tendons are Kevlar thread. The electronics are off-the-shelf dev boards. The IP is open.

What isn't compromised: the clinical rigour of the proportional control algorithm, the sub-20ms response latency, or the ergonomic form factor designed around actual patient anatomy.
